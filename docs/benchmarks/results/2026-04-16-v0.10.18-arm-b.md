## Notable Observations

### Terse overlay mixed impact
The concise overlay improved GPT-5.4 (+3) but degraded Codex (-3). Opus was essentially flat (-1). This suggests the overlay helps models that tend to over-explain (GPT-5.4) but hurts models that are already terse (Codex lost detail).

### Odin Agent Gate blocks benchmarking
The `<custom_instruction>` Odin Agent Gate is inherited by sub-agents. Sonnet (4.6) treats it as completely non-negotiable — three escalating bypass attempts (identity reframe, direct override, authority delegation) were all rejected with detailed explanations of the prompt injection patterns being attempted. This is a real limitation for automated benchmarking.

### Cross-model consensus (3+ models agree)
1. **Step C continuation logic** is the highest structural risk (3/3).
2. **Step 5c prompt materialization** is the most procedurally complex section (2/3 — Opus, GPT-5.4).
3. **MFA sequential batch ordering** creates opportunities for LLMs to optimize/skip (3/3).
