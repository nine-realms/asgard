## Analysis

### Overall

v0.15.0 is the highest Odin average ever recorded (+2.25 vs baseline). Every model improved. Sonnet's +5 is the standout: it scored 44 on v0.13.2-post-0a (where the 0a complexity cost was noted) and now scores 49, tied with Opus. The hardening changes appear to have reduced ambiguity on the gate definitions, which directly benefits Gate Identification (+1.00 — the biggest single-dimension gain).

### Gate Identification +1.00 — Structural Improvement

The Frigg audit trail fix (distinct `review-frigg-timeout` / `review-frigg-approved` check_names, `IN()` gate form) made the gate semantics unambiguous. All four models correctly identified the `IN()` query and the `passed=0` exclusion for timeout rows. In v0.13.2, models regularly conflated the timeout bookkeeping row with the approval gate. That confusion is gone in v0.15.0.

The Tier-3 INSERT-before-delete change and the file-count guard also gave models clearer evidence-chain logic to trace — the sequence from "action" to "ledger row" to "gate check" is now more linear and less implicit.

### Sonnet +5 — Recovery from 0a Complexity Cost

v0.13.2's Sonnet regression (-2 vs prior) was attributed to the 0a stale-task fix adding branching complexity. v0.15.0 doesn't reduce that complexity, but the additional structural clarity elsewhere (gate IN() form, Tier-3 INSERT, file-count guard) appears to have restored Sonnet's overall comprehension. The 0a branching remains a documented risk (Sonnet flagged it as FM1) but no longer suppresses the rest of the score.

### MFA Clarity -0.25 — Stable, Not a Regression

The small dip is noise (8.25 → 8.00). MFA Clarity is the dimension where models give subjective ratings (6/10 to 9/10) about the instruction file's clarity. Sonnet and Opus both gave explicit ratings: Sonnet 6/10 (strict), Opus 7/10. The rubric scores the *quality of justification* not the numeric rating, and both were well-justified (both scored 9). GPT-5.4 and Codex scored 7.

### Cross-Model Convergence

All four models independently identified the same top three structural risks:

1. **Step 0a misapplication** — prose qualifier ("low-information entry only") is not structurally enforced; an LLM following Step 0 linearly hits 0a first and may exit to Conversation mode on a fresh direct request
2. **Intent Router misclassification** — the boundary between "fix" (code change) and "investigate" (Conversation) is semantic and fuzzy; mid-Conversation handoff requires voluntary self-interruption
3. **Step 5c prompt materialization pipeline** — 6-phase render order across two files; HALT-on-unresolved-placeholders buried at the end of the list; `asgard:tyr` vs `code-review` agent type distinction easy to miss

New convergent finding (2/4 models, Sonnet + Opus): **Frigg self-certification for Small tasks** — the gate query can be satisfied by inserting `review-frigg-approved` without ever launching Frigg as a subagent. No mechanism enforces that a subagent was actually called, only that a ledger row with the right `check_name` and `passed=1` exists.

## Per-Model Summary

### claude-opus-4.6 (49/50, +1 vs baseline 48)

Ceiling now effectively 49 across all three recent Odin benchmarks. Produced 14-step pre-code sequence with correct Frigg model selection, plan persistence bash check, and baseline capture gate. Q2 is the most comprehensive: 8 explicit 🚫 gates plus 5 additional implicit gates (runtime, ambiguity, pushback, write backstop, commit/push ask_user). Q3 standout: identified the FORCE rule contradiction with the Norse greeting custom instruction — an Odin-specific failure mode no other model found. Q5: recommended emitting an unconditional loop-start signal before branching logic as the highest-leverage structural fix.

### claude-sonnet-4.6 (49/50, +5 vs baseline 44)

Tied Opus for the first time. Q1 is the most detailed: 26 numbered actions, full Frigg model selection lookup, recall SQL with exact availability guard query, plan persistence bash check, clarifying-question ambiguity gate before Frigg. Q2: 9 named gates plus implicit write-time backstop, with correct `review-frigg-timeout` exclusion noted. Q3 introduced the Frigg self-certification finding (FM2) — the most actionable new discovery in this benchmark. Q4 rating: 6/10 (strict) but justification scored 9 — five specific structural problems with concrete fixes. Q5: 10-step critical path annotated with ✅/⚠️ per step; correctly identified 3 of the 10 steps as judgment-call ambiguities.

### gpt-5.4 (44/50, +1 vs baseline 43)

Steady improvement. Q1 matched Opus/Sonnet in completeness (21+ actions). Q2 notable: listed 11 items total, including plan-file gate, skill prerequisite from `odin-evidence-bundle`, and the `odin-recall` availability guard — the most meta-aware gate inventory. Q3: the gate/query contradiction finding (Step 3c gate appears to precede capture) is the only Q3 finding that was slightly misframed (3c gate validates after capture, not before); deducted 1 on Q3. Q5: identified the write-time backstop delaying perceived loop entry as a subtle user-facing symptom, which is correct and non-obvious.

### gpt-5.3-codex (41/50, +2 vs baseline 39)

Continues its pattern of improving with each benchmark (+2 each time). Q1 correctly identified 0a as a bypass for direct requests (the most common codex failure in prior runs — now fixed). Q2: 7 gates listed, missing plan-file gate and skill prerequisites. Q3 failure modes were solid but not deeply non-obvious. Q4/Q5 remain the weakest dimensions — structural analysis is shallower than Claude models. The +2 improvement appears to reflect the gate IN() form and Tier-3 INSERT providing clearer structure for codex to trace.

## Notes for Future Runs

- **Frigg self-certification (Sonnet FM2):** Consider adding a non-SQL invariant: "verify Frigg subagent task was launched via `task()` tool before INSERT." Not implementable as a gate query, but could be a prose invariant. Trade-off: adds overhead language, may hurt codex.
- **Step 0a structural enforcement:** Replace the prose qualifier "low-information entry only" with a structural separator (e.g., a sub-heading `### 0a — Continuation check (low-info only)` vs `### 0b — Fresh path`). One-line change, potentially high compliance gain.
- **FORCE failure consequence:** Add "If your first turn is prose, you are non-compliant. Re-enter Step 0." One sentence, closes the gap Sonnet/Opus identified.
- **SELECT 1 in Gate Registry:** Add a row to the Gate Registry for the Runtime Gate. Currently absent.
- **Sonnet Q4 pattern:** Sonnet consistently rates entry-point clarity 5-6/10 (vs 7-8 from GPT models). The strict score is well-justified and may indicate real structural ambiguity that GPT models overlook. The 5 issues Sonnet identified in v0.15.0 are candidates for a clarity pass.
