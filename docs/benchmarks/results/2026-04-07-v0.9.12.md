## Analysis

### Opus new all-time high (49)
Opus's best-ever showing is driven by deeper analytical output, not by the file becoming easier. The v0.9.12 additions (more gates, more exceptions, more conditional paths) gave Opus more surface area to analyze, and it responded with the most detailed 5c materialization failure analysis seen in any benchmark — identifying specific ordering violations, Mimir prompt variance by size, and the unresolved token scan skip. The top-of-file intercept proposal in Loop Start is also the most concrete structural fix suggestion from any model to date.

### Codex dip (-1)
Codex dropped from 47 to 46 on MFA Clarity (9→8). The response was more compressed overall — less detail across all dimensions compared to v0.9.11's breakout performance. The MFA analysis lacked the specific structural observations that earned 9 previously (e.g., recognition of reinforcement language addressing its processing pattern). This appears to be natural response variation rather than a regression caused by v0.9.12 changes — all new features (timeout gates, plan approval, branch reuse) were correctly identified in Sequence and Gates.

### New gates absorbed cleanly
All 4 models correctly identified and enumerated the v0.9.12 additions: Frigg timeout acceptance in the gate query, mandatory post-Frigg plan approval, branch-reuse detection with PR-feedback carve-out, and user-provided plan exception for Step 3b. No model was confused by the additional conditional paths, suggesting the prose is clear despite adding ~12 lines to an already long file.

### MFA Clarity plateau
All 4 models score 8-9 on MFA Clarity for the third consecutive version. The remaining gap is structural — forward references, "do not read further" being unenforceable for transformer inference, and competing system prompt directives. These are not fixable with prose alone. Opus's report_intent chicken-and-egg observation and the top-of-file intercept proposal are the most actionable suggestions for breaking through the 8-9 ceiling.

### Convergent novel insights
Two novel observations surfaced independently by 2+ models (high-confidence):
- **Step 5c materialization ordering is the highest-risk execution block** (Opus + Sonnet both provide detailed sub-failure analysis)
- **"Do not read further" governs execution order, not parsing order** — LLMs process the full system prompt before acting (Sonnet + GPT-5.4 + Codex)

Single-model novel observations worth investigating:
- **Opus:** report_intent chicken-and-egg with system prompt rules; top-of-file intercept directive
- **Sonnet:** Investigation classification as "likely culprit" for perceived loop non-start; "no gate until Step 8" if model skips both MFA and Loop
- **GPT-5.4:** Plan/Frigg/approval branching complexity increased by v0.9.12 — more conditional paths mean more opportunities for misrouting
