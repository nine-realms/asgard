## Analysis

### Compression succeeded — net positive across the panel

v0.10.8 removed 100 lines (−12%) and gained +0.50 average score (45.75→46.25). This is the highest average since v0.10.6 (46.0), achieved with 70 fewer lines. The compression-to-score ratio confirms the hypothesis: shorter, tighter prose improves processability without losing logic.

### Sonnet MFA clarity recovered from 6→7

The primary target of v0.10.8 was Sonnet's MFA clarity regression in v0.10.7 (9→6). The compression brought it to 7/10 — a partial recovery. Sonnet's remaining complaints are structural rather than density-related:

1. **Conditional logic inside MFA** — steps 4–5 being conditional on step 3's output creates a branch at the entry point
2. **"First batch" scope ambiguity** — "first batch" could be read as "first group of MFA steps" not "first tool call"
3. **No hard visual separator** between MFA and the Loop (both are H2)
4. **Subjective continuation criteria** — "stays in scope" has no objective signal
5. **`phase = 'after'` for loop-entry** — semantically surprising

These are deeper structural issues that prose compression alone can't fix. Getting to 8+ would require restructuring the MFA flow itself.

### GPT-5.4 improved +2 points (44→46)

The biggest single-model gain in the panel. GPT-5.4's Failure Mode analysis improved (finding size-escalation backtrack compliance as a novel issue), and its Loop Start analysis was more thorough. The shorter file appears to help GPT models process the full spec more carefully.

### Opus dropped 1 point (49→48)

Opus's MFA rating held at 8/10 (same as v0.10.7... actually Opus gave 9/10 in v0.10.6). The transition sentence "MFA complete. New tasks → begin the Odin Loop at Step 0" was flagged as easy to blur past. Opus's total drop from 49→48 is within noise range and offset by GPT-5.4's +2.

### Codex stable at 44

Codex rated MFA 8.5/10 (scored as 9), consistent with its v0.10.7 rating. No regression from compression.

## Cross-Model Consensus (v0.10.8)

### Unanimous findings (4/4 models)

1. **MFA bypass remains the #1 failure mode** — all four models identify "LLM skips MFA and jumps to Step 0/implementation" as the highest risk
2. **Step 5c prompt materialization is the most complex procedure** — 6-phase render order, conditional blocks, model variable resolution across two files
3. **Continuation logic has subjective criteria** — "stays in scope" and "no new file/feature/bug" require judgment calls with no objective signals
4. **Step 0 loop-entry gate is self-referential** — only works if the LLM runs it, but an LLM that skipped MFA is unlikely to run the gate

### Majority findings (3/4 models)

5. **MFA conditional branching at initialization is risky** — steps 4–5 being conditional on step 3 creates a decision tree at the entry point (Opus, Sonnet, GPT-5.4)
6. **"First batch" phrasing could be misread** — ambiguity between "first tool call" and "first group of steps" (Sonnet, GPT-5.4, Codex)

### Novel findings (single model)

7. **Size escalation backtrack has no gate** (Sonnet) — When a task escalates from Small→Large mid-planning, the instruction says to re-run Steps 1b and 2 at the new depth, but no gate verifies this happened. The reviewer-count gates enforce the right number of reviewers but not the right depth of input analysis.
8. **Stale task_id from prior session** (Sonnet) — If `odin_checks` persists from a prior session with a stale `loop-entry` row, the Step 0 gate could pass incorrectly. The MFA self-audit instruction says "did I complete all 5 MFA steps *this turn*?" but this is implicit in the gate, not explicit.

## Remaining Structural Issues (not addressable by further compression)

These are findings that persist across v0.10.4–v0.10.8 and would require structural changes:

1. **First-batch constraint is unenforceable** — training-vs-instruction tension (all versions)
2. **Continuation scope checks are subjective** — no objective signals (all versions)
3. **Step 0 has 10+ sub-steps** — gate fatigue risk (since v0.10.4)
4. **Skill invocation timing fragile at Step 5c distance** — skill loaded mid-step (since v0.10.5)
5. **Size escalation backtrack has no verification gate** — new finding in v0.10.8
6. **Stale task_id cross-session leakage** — new finding in v0.10.8
