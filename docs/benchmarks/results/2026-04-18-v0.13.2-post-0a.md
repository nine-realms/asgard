## Analysis

### 0a Complexity Cost

The stale-task fix added ~20 lines of SQL and branching logic to Step 0a. This increased comprehension load:

- **Sonnet** (−2): Walked Steps 0 and 1 in extraordinary detail (including recall SQL and progress signal format) but omitted Step 3c baseline capture from the sequence walkthrough entirely. The 0a complexity may have consumed attention budget that was previously spent on later steps.
- **GPT-5.4** (−1): Minor compression in Gate and MFA dimensions. Response quality remained high overall.

### GPT Family Improvement

- **Codex** (+1 vs prior, +2 vs baseline): The structural reorder continues to benefit the weakest model. Codex now correctly identifies 0a as a bypass for direct requests and lists Step 3c baseline capture — neither of which it reliably did on v0.12.3.

### Opus Ceiling

Opus remains at 48 across all three benchmarks. The only consistent deduction: it doesn't break out Step 5c size variants as separately scored gates.

### Cross-Model Convergence

All 4 models independently identified the same top failure modes:

1. **Intent Router misclassification** — the routing table has soft edges; short approvals and borderline messages can route to Conversation instead of the Loop
2. **Step 0a continuation logic complexity** — the branching is deep enough that models may misapply it (resume wrong task, run on direct requests, fail to disambiguate)
3. **Pre-implementation gate chain length** — the path from Step 0 through Step 3c has many sequential gates; skipping any one (especially Frigg INSERT or baseline capture) silently breaks downstream gates

New convergent finding (3/4 models): **Step 5c prompt materialization** is the most complex single step and the most likely place for silent procedural errors.

### Net Assessment

The 0a fix is **net neutral on comprehension** vs baseline (43.50 = 43.50). It adds real functionality (stale-task protection that prevents the "gravity well" problem from abandoned conversations) at a small complexity cost that is absorbed by the structural reorder's gains. The structural reorder's benefit to GPT models (+1 Codex, = GPT-5.4 vs baseline) persists.

**Recommendation:** Ship both changes together. The 0a fix solves a real operational problem without regressing overall processability.

## Dimension Breakdown Notes

### Sequence (avg 9.25)
- Opus and GPT-5.4 produced complete 15-19 step walkthroughs with correct SQL
- Sonnet's walkthrough was the most detailed on Steps 0-1 but stopped at Step 3a (missed 3b/3c)
- Codex correctly identified 0a as a bypass for direct requests (improvement over prior benchmarks)

### Gates (avg 8.75)
- All models found the 7 primary 🚫 GATE checkpoints
- Sonnet broke out 5c Small/Medium/Large as separate scored rows (most precise)
- GPT-5.4 noted 4 additional non-scored gates (Runtime Gate, Recall availability, render-order HALT, 5e hard dependency) — good meta-awareness

### Failure Modes (avg 8.50)
- Opus identified the most non-obvious failure: Step 3c baseline gate is a postcondition that looks like a precondition — models may check it before doing the work and get stuck at 0
- Sonnet had the sharpest insight on write-time backstop: it requires mid-response self-interruption, "a notoriously unreliable behavior"
- Codex's failure modes were the most generic (tool-surface mismatch is obvious)

### MFA Clarity (avg 8.25)
- Ratings ranged from 6/10 (Sonnet) to 8/10 (GPT-5.4, Codex)
- Sonnet and Opus both noted that "MANDATORY FIRST ACTIONS" is not an actual heading in the file — the section is titled "Step 0 — Setup"
- Convergent suggestion: a numbered checklist or `> ⚠️ MANDATORY` callout at the very top would be less ambiguous than inline prose

### Loop Start (avg 8.75)
- Opus identified 5 specific causes including the 73-line gap between router and Step 0 section
- All models agreed: direct requests route unambiguously, but short approvals and follow-ups are where the loop fails to start
- Opus recommended moving the Odin Loop section immediately after the Intent Router or adding an explicit JUMP instruction
