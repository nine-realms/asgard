## Change Summary

v0.10.18 adds an early user-facing signal (`👁️ Odin peers into the Well of Mímir…`) in MFA Step E, right after loop-entry verification. The change also updates the prose exception list to allow pushback callouts before the start signal (Mimir review finding).

---

## Notable Observations

### Early signal correctly processed by all models
All 4 models identified the new early signal in their Q1 first-turn sequences, placing it correctly after MFA-E loop-entry verification and before Step 0. No model confused it with the existing start signal (`🔁 Odin Loop — {task_id} | {size} | Starting...`).

### Two-signal progression noted as both feature and risk
- **claude-opus-4.6** and **claude-sonnet-4.6** identified the dual-signal pattern as a potential source of user confusion in Q5 — the gap between `👁️` (MFA-E) and `🔁` (Step 0, post-sizing) can include `ask_user` pauses that look like stalls.
- **gpt-5.4** noted the two signals can look inconsistent and the startup can pause between them.
- **gpt-5.3-codex** mentioned the two signals as a failure mode contributor.

### gpt-5.4 Q4 regression (-1): MFA clarity rating dropped
gpt-5.4 rated MFA clarity 7/10 (vs implied 8/10 in v0.10.17) with thinner structural justification. The early signal didn't cause this — the model's analysis was simply less detailed this run. Within normal variance for a single-point drop.

### Cross-model consensus findings (3+ models agree)
1. **Step C continuation logic remains the highest structural risk** (4/4) — all models flag the branching complexity and `{task_id}` discard rule.
2. **Dual-signal startup ambiguity** (4/4) — all models noted the early signal + start signal creates a two-phase startup where `ask_user` gates can fire between them, confusing users about whether the loop started.
3. **Step 5c prompt materialization fragility** (2/4 — Opus, Sonnet) — the 6-phase render pipeline in odin-review-prompts SKILL.md is error-prone for LLMs.
