## Analysis

### Codex breakthrough (+5)
The loop-bypass reinforcement had an outsized impact on Codex. The identity-level invariant ("Every code-change task — no matter how trivial — goes through the Odin Loop in full") and the pre-edit gate ("If you are about to call `edit`, `create`, or run a write command in `bash` without a `loop-entry` row...") directly address Codex's processing pattern: it scans for actionable anchors. The explicit "this is the most common failure mode" callout gave Codex a named pattern to avoid, rather than an implicit norm to intuit.

Codex now ties Opus at 47 — unprecedented. The 8-point gap from v0.9.5 run 1 (37) to v0.9.11 (47) represents the cumulative effect of: bold labels (v0.9.10), `🚫 GATE:` standardization (v0.9.10), Gate Registry (v0.9.10), and now loop-bypass reinforcement (v0.9.11).

### Perfect Sequence and Gates
First benchmark where all 4 models score 10/10 on both Sequence and Gates. The combination of standardized gate formatting, Gate Registry, Step 0 sub-action labels, and the reinforcement language means every model can now reliably enumerate the full critical path. The "how to process this file" has been solved — remaining variation is in analytical depth (Failure Modes, Loop Start).

### MFA Clarity split
Opus and Codex moved to 9; Sonnet and GPT-5.4 held at 8. The reinforcement helped models that were borderline (Codex at 8, Opus at 8) but didn't move models that were already confident in their compliance assessment. This suggests diminishing returns on MFA prose clarity — the remaining gap is structural (forward references, soft transitions) not textual.

### Sonnet ceiling
Sonnet has been at 48 for two versions. The remaining 2 points are MFA Clarity (8) — Sonnet's critique of "do not read further" being unenforceable and Step 5 being a soft instruction is legitimate and not fixable with prose alone. 48 may be the practical ceiling without structural changes to MFA.

### Reviewer insights worth considering
- **Sonnet:** Suggested a distinct `loop-start` INSERT (separate from `loop-entry`) to make the MFA→Loop transition auditable. Would close the "invisible startup" gap.
- **GPT-5.4:** Identified that "do not read further" contradicts Step 2's need to reference the CREATE TABLE from the Verification Ledger section. Novel structural observation.
- **Opus:** Noted that `report_intent` is referenced but never defined as a tool — potential confusion for models that don't have it.
