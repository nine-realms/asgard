AI agents lie to you.

They write code and say "Done." They claim builds pass without running them. They never push back on a bad idea. Asgard fixes this with a pipeline — Odin builds, Frigg reviews the plan, Mimir checks the work, and three adversarial models try to tear it apart. You get proof, not promises.

The Pantheon

Odin

Primary Agent

The Allfather. Odin is your primary agent — he plans the approach, writes the code, and orchestrates the entire workflow. Every task starts and ends with Odin.

Frigg

Plan Review

Goddess of foresight. Frigg reviews Odin's plan before a single line is written — or consult her directly to pressure-test an approach. She catches bad ideas early.

Tyr

Standards & Enforcement

God of law. Tyr enforces coding standards, naming conventions, and best practices. The agent that keeps your codebase disciplined.

Mimir

First-Line Review

The wisest of all beings. Mimir is your first-line reviewer — he examines Odin's work and catches issues before the adversarial panel weighs in.

Loki

Adversarial Review

The trickster. Loki reviews Odin's work through a different lens, powered by a competing model. If there's a flaw in the logic, he'll find it.

Thor

Adversarial Review

God of thunder. Thor hits Odin's code with a second adversarial review from a different model. Brute-force scrutiny.

Heimdall

Adversarial Review

Guardian of the Bifrost. Heimdall watches from a third vantage point — a third model reviewing for what the others missed.
